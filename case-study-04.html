The problem.

Clandestina 74, a fine dining restaurant in the heart of Guanajuato's historic centre, had a problem that never showed up on the balance sheet. During their busiest nights — Friday through Sunday — the team was so occupied serving guests that nobody could pick up the phone.

People calling to make a reservation heard ringing, then nothing. So they hung up and called somewhere else. The owner had a full dining room and felt like business was good — but he had no idea how much money was walking out the door through unanswered calls.

When we sat down together and actually counted it — tracking call volume, average party size, and average ticket — the number was staggering: between MXN $20,000 and $50,000 in lost revenue every single week. Not because the food was bad. Not because the service was slow. Simply because the phone rang and nobody could answer it.

"We didn't even realize how many reservations we were losing until they showed us the data. The phone was our biggest leak — and we never knew."
— Owner, Clandestina 74

The approach.

We built a smart system that works like a dedicated reservations assistant — one that never gets busy, never takes a day off, and handles everything automatically.

When a customer calls, the AI picks up and has a natural conversation in Spanish. It checks table availability against Clandestina 74's live seating data, suggests alternative times if the preferred slot is full, handles party size logistics, and confirms the booking — all without any staff involvement.

But we didn't stop at reservations. One to two days after each visit, the system automatically sends each guest a friendly WhatsApp or text message asking how their experience was — with a direct link to leave a Google review. This turned a passive hope ("maybe they'll review us") into an active, automated pipeline of 5-star reviews.

Delivery timeline

Week 1 / Audit + Design
Spent three evenings in the restaurant during peak service. Tracked call volume, measured missed calls, and calculated the revenue leak. Designed the voice agent conversation flow with the owner, mapping every scenario: walk-ins, large parties, special occasions, dietary requests.
Week 2 / Build + Train
Built the AI voice agent with natural Spanish conversation capabilities. Connected to the existing reservation book (migrated from paper to a simple digital system). Built the availability checking logic and the WhatsApp post-visit follow-up sequence. Trained the model on 50+ scripted scenarios.
Week 3 / Test + Launch
Ran the system in parallel for 4 days — every call went to AI and a staff member listened in. Zero booking errors. Customers couldn't tell it wasn't a person. Went fully live on Thursday before the weekend rush.

The ripple effect.

The most unexpected impact wasn't the rescued revenue or the Google reviews — it was staff morale. The front-of-house team told us they used to dread the phone ringing during a packed Friday night. Now they can focus entirely on the guests in front of them.

The owner is now exploring a second phase: using the AI system to handle catering inquiries and private event bookings, which currently require 3-4 back-and-forth calls to finalize. The same architecture scales directly.

From a business that didn't know it was leaking money, to one that captures every single opportunity, 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, without hiring a single additional person.
