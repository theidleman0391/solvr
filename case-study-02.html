The problem.

AMG Abogados is a mid-market firm handling litigation, corporate advisory, and compliance across 14 practice areas. Their inbox was a warzone. Around 200 emails per day from various sectors or clients, flowing into shared mailboxes, each needing to be read, classified by urgency and practice area, and routed to the right attorney.

Their existing process relied on three full-time paralegals who did nothing but read and forward emails. Urgent matters got buried. Routine questions were escalated to senior partners. Conflicts-of-interest checks happened after the fact — or not at all.

The average time from email received to first attorney response was 4.7 hours. For a firm that bills by responsiveness, that delay was costing them clients.

"We had three people whose entire job was to read emails and decide who should actually read them. That's not a process — that's purgatory."
— Managing Partner, AMG Abogados

The approach.

This wasn't a "throw GPT at an inbox" project. Legal email triage has hard constraints: confidentiality, accuracy, and auditability. We designed a multi-stage LangGraph pipeline where every decision is traceable and every classification is logged.

The core classification model uses Claude 3.5 Sonnet with a carefully tuned system prompt and 14 practice-area definitions. We augmented it with a RAG layer trained on 18 months of historically-routed emails, so the model understands AMG Abogados' specific client relationships and routing preferences.

Every email passes through a conflict-of-interest detector that cross-references sender identity, mentioned parties, and case numbers against an internal database. This alone caught 23 potential conflicts in the first month — issues that would have been missed under the manual process.

Delivery timeline

Weeks 1–2 / Audit
Analyzed 90 days of email traffic across all practice areas. Built a classification taxonomy with AMG Abogados' senior partners. Identified 14 primary categories and 47 sub-categories.
Weeks 3–4 / Design + Eval
Designed the LangGraph pipeline. Built an evaluation suite of 500 labeled emails. Established accuracy thresholds: 95% minimum classification, 99% conflict detection.
Weeks 5–6 / Build
Implemented the full agent graph with Claude 3.5 Sonnet, conflict detection, priority scoring, and Gmail integration. Built guardrails for low-confidence classifications — anything below 85% confidence routes to a human reviewer.
Weeks 7–8 / Shadow + Ship
Ran in shadow mode for 10 days alongside the manual team. Accuracy: 96.4%. Zero missed urgent items. Deployed to production with monitoring dashboard for partners.

The result.

Within 60 days, AMG Abogados reassigned all three triage paralegals to substantive casework — work that actually requires a human brain. The managing partner estimated the reallocation added $340K in annual recoverable billing capacity.

Client satisfaction scores jumped 18%. Partners now receive a weekly analytics report showing email volume trends, response time distributions, and emerging client issues — data they'd never had before.

The system has processed over 72,000 emails without a single missed urgent item. It runs 24/7, including weekends and holidays — something the manual team never could.
