The problem.

Mercado Verde is a direct-to-consumer marketplace specializing in organic produce and artisanal goods, serving Mexico City, Guadalajara, and Monterrey. They sell through six distinct channels — their own Shopify storefront, MercadoLibre, Amazon Mexico, Rappi, Cornershop, and a wholesale portal for restaurants.

The problem was devastatingly simple: they had no real-time inventory visibility across channels. Each platform maintained its own stock count. When a customer bought the last case of organic honey on Shopify, MercadoLibre still showed it in stock — and sold it again.

Overselling had become the defining operational crisis. In peak months, they were canceling 12-15% of orders. Customer refund requests were consuming two full-time coordinators. Their supplier relationships were fraying because they couldn't distinguish real demand from phantom demand.

"We'd sell out of avocados on Amazon at 8am and then sell them again on Rappi at 9am. By 10am we had angry customers and no avocados."
— Head of Operations, Mercado Verde

Six channels, one truth.

Each channel had different APIs, different rate limits, different data models. Unifying them required a system that could handle the lowest common denominator while preserving channel-specific logic.

The approach.

We chose Temporal as the workflow engine because inventory sync is a distributed systems problem disguised as a simple CRUD operation. Orders arrive asynchronously from six channels. Stock reservations need to be atomic. Failures need to be retried without double-counting. Temporal gave us durable execution with saga-pattern rollbacks out of the box.

When an order is placed on any channel, a webhook fires into our ingestion layer. Temporal picks up the event, decrements the central stock in BigQuery, then propagates the updated count to all five other channels. The saga pattern ensures that if any channel's API fails to update, the system retries with exponential backoff — and if it truly fails, rolls back the change and alerts the ops team.

We also built a stock reservation system: when a customer adds an item to their cart on any channel, we hold a soft reservation for 15 minutes. This prevents the race condition where two customers on different platforms "buy" the last unit simultaneously.

Delivery timeline

Weeks 1–2 / Map
Audited all six channel APIs. Documented rate limits, webhook payloads, and inventory data models. Every channel was different — MercadoLibre uses XML, Rappi uses GraphQL, Amazon FBA has its own inventory management.
Weeks 3–4 / Design
Designed the canonical inventory schema in BigQuery. Built the Temporal workflow graph with saga-pattern compensation. Defined the channel adapter interface — each channel gets its own adapter that normalizes data to our schema.
Weeks 5–7 / Build
Built six channel adapters in Python. Implemented the Temporal workflows with retry policies, dead-letter queues, and the soft-reservation system. Load-tested with synthetic traffic simulating 2× peak volume.
Weeks 8–9 / Stage + Test
Ran in shadow mode alongside the manual process for 12 days. Caught and fixed 4 edge cases: Amazon FBA split shipments, MercadoLibre promotional holds, Cornershop substitution logic, and wholesale minimum-order quantities.
Week 10 / Ship
Cut over to production. Killed the spreadsheet process. Zero oversells on day one. Zero since.

The result.

Stock-outs dropped by 83%. Order cancellations due to overselling went from 12-15% to zero. Literally zero — the system has not oversold a single item since launch.

The two coordinators who spent their days processing refunds and apology emails were reassigned to supplier development and new channel expansion. Mercado Verde is now evaluating two additional channels (Uber Eats and iFood) — something that would have been operationally impossible before.

The BigQuery analytics layer also unlocked something unexpected: cross-channel demand intelligence. For the first time, Mercado Verde can see that organic honey sells 3× faster on Rappi than MercadoLibre, and adjust pricing and inventory allocation dynamically.

"We used to dread holidays because of oversells. Last Buen Fin we processed 14,000 orders across all six channels with zero cancellations. That's never happened before."
— CEO, Mercado Verde
